üèóÔ∏è Understanding the Wholesaling Framework

Wholesaling is essentially being a middleman in real estate transactions. You're not buying properties‚Äîyou're securing the right to buy them through contracts, then selling that right to end buyers for a fee.

üìä Typical Workflow:

  1. Find a distressed property or motivated seller
  2. Get the property under contract at below-market price
  3. Market the contract to your cash buyer network
  4. Assign the contract or double-close
  5. Collect your assignment fee ($5,000-$20,000+ per deal)

‚öñÔ∏è Georgia Legal Compliance: The Non-Negotiables

üö® Licensing Reality

Georgia doesn't require a real estate license for wholesaling if you're selling your own equitable interest in a contract. However, you cannot market properties you don't have under contract, and you cannot represent others in negotiations.

üìã Essential Legal Documents:

  • Purchase and Sale Agreement (your contract with seller)
  • Assignment of Contract Agreement (transfers your rights to buyer)
  • Memorandum of Contract (optional but protects your position)
  • Disclosure statements (always disclose you're an investor)
